# Academic Writing Guide

## Paper Structure (IMRaD)

### Abstract (150-300 words)

* **Background**: 1-2 sentences.
* **Purpose**: 1 sentence.
* **Methods**: 2-3 sentences.
* **Results**: 2-3 sentences.
* **Conclusions**: 1-2 sentences.

### Introduction (Funnel Structure)

1. **Broad context**: Why this matters.
2. **Narrow focus**: Specific problem.
3. **Gap**: What is unknown.
4. **Research question**: Investigation goal.
5. **Contribution**: Why it matters.

### Methods

**Reproducibility Test**: Can it be replicated?

* Participants/Sample.
* Materials/Measures.
* Procedure.
* Analysis approach.
* Ethical considerations.

### Results

**Report, don't interpret**:

* Systematic presentation.
* Tables/Figures for complex data.
* Effect sizes + p-values.

### Discussion (Reverse Funnel)

1. Summary of findings.
2. Interpretation in context.
3. Theoretical implications.
4. Practical implications.
5. Limitations.
6. Future directions.
7. Conclusion.

## Writing Style

### Voice and Tense

* **Abstract**: Past (methods/results), Present (conclusions).
* **Introduction**: Present (established knowledge).
* **Methods/Results**: Past.
* **Discussion**: Present + Past.

### Common Pitfalls

* **Wordiness**: Use "Results demonstrate" instead of "It is important to note that the results demonstrate".
* **Passive Voice**: Use "We found" instead of "It was found".
* **Vague language**: Be specific (cite sources).
* **Hedging**: Use "suggests" or "indicates" instead of "proves".

## Grant Proposals (NSF/NIH Style)

* **Specific Aims**: Problem, gap, goal, objectives, hypothesis, impact.
* **Significance**: Importance, gaps, advancement.
* **Innovation**: Conceptual, methodological, technical.
* **Approach**: Rationale, design, methods, outcomes.
